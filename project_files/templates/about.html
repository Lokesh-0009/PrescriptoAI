SYSTEM OVERVIEW

How PrescriptoAI Works

From a handwritten prescription image to a complete clinical intelligence report — here's the pipeline:

01
OCR Extraction
Upload a prescription image. The TrOCR vision transformer reads handwritten text with high accuracy.
02
NLP Entity Extraction
Fuzzy-matching NLP identifies medicines, symptoms, and dosage patterns from the raw OCR text.
03
Disease Prediction
A rule-based engine scores risks across 10 disease categories using symptom and medication indicators.
04
Safety Analysis
Drug interaction checks and dosage safety validation flag potential risks before dispensing.
KEY FEATURES

Intelligent Clinical Tools

Purpose-built modules that work together to deliver comprehensive prescription intelligence.

Handwriting Recognition

Microsoft TrOCR transformer model reads handwritten prescriptions and converts them to digital text with high fidelity.

Disease Risk Prediction

Analyzes symptoms and medication patterns to predict risks for diabetes, hypertension, heart disease, infections, and more — with confidence scores.

Drug Interaction Detection

Cross-references prescribed medicines against a database of known interactions, flagging minor, moderate, and major severity conflicts.

Dosage Safety Validation

Compares prescribed doses against safe daily limits for 20+ common medications, alerting when doses approach or exceed thresholds.

Audit Trail & Logging

Every prescription analysis, user action, and system event is logged with timestamps for compliance, accountability, and security monitoring.

Role-Based Access Control

Admin and pharmacist roles with separate dashboards, management capabilities, and permission-gated features for secure hospital workflows.

Real-World Healthcare Impact

Medication errors affect millions of patients worldwide every year. PrescriptoAI addresses this challenge at the point of prescribing — where most errors originate. By automating the reading, analysis, and safety-checking of prescriptions, the system acts as an intelligent second opinion for pharmacists and clinicians, reducing dispensing errors, catching dangerous drug combinations, and providing evidence-based disease risk indicators — all within seconds of scanning a prescription.
